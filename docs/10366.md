# 给第一次发言人的提示

> 原文:[https://dev . to/emmabostian/tips-for-first-time-speakers-3fcb](https://dev.to/emmabostian/tips-for-first-time-speakers-3fcb)

我刚刚接受了我的第一次会议演讲，当兴奋稍微平息后，我开始紧张起来。我以前在当地集会上演讲过，但想到我将站在 1000 多人面前演讲，我的焦虑加剧了。

因此，我向社区寻求成功的会议演讲技巧！下面，我从社区中收集了一些我最喜欢的回复。

# [](#preparation)准备

一个好的会议演讲始于勤奋的准备。

> ![Cindy Alvarez profile image](../Images/66224443ba44cbb226a7886ac724e9c4.png)辛迪阿尔瓦雷斯@ cindyalvarez![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ kvlly](https://twitter.com/kvlly)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)就这一点而言——人们认为他们应该背熟自己的演讲，或者接近它，所以你在舞台上的声音是经过打磨的。不要！那些谈话听起来很生硬，任何差异都会让你不知所措。
> 
> 但是前 20 秒一定要多多练习。如果顺利推出，其他事情就容易多了。2019 年 3 月 13 日下午 14:46[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105842508821483520)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105842508821483520)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105842508821483520)

Memorizing your talk is important, however you don't want to sound as if you're reading from a script. So it's important to memorize just the key points of your talk. This way, you'll sound prepared without sounding rehearsed.

> ![Ricky profile image](../Images/2317a24c48c5e102842530ac7bc07a44.png)瑞奇[@ rickhanlonii](https://dev.to/rickhanlonii)![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)我的诀窍是做一吨幻灯片，像 20 分钟 250-300 张。这让观众参与进来，但更重要的是让我保持在正确的轨道上，以坚实的步伐在演讲中前进
> 
> 如果忘记了要说什么，那么*下一张幻灯片*下午 12:16-2019 年 3 月 13 日[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105804879270940672)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105804879270940672)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105804879270940672)

One of the quickest and easiest ways to keep your audience engaged is by having a lot of slides with a small amount of content on each. When you put too much content on a slide, the audience forgets to listen to you and instead focuses on reading the slide. Thus, having many slides not only keeps the audience engaged, but keeps the pace of your talk moving.

> ![Adam Riley profile image](../Images/895c3596e03cb514da801a29af192303.png)亚当·莱利@ cppriley![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)我已经开始做的事情是在我真正演讲的几周前预订一个较小的真实听众。通常与用户组或在客户端。给我一个期限，这样我不会把幻灯片留到最后一分钟；以及现场练习和在当天之前获得反馈的机会。2019 年 3 月 13 日下午 13:08[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105817979374264321)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105817979374264321)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105817979374264321)

Doing a practice run for a smaller audience is always a great idea. This way you can get all of your nerves out before the big day. This also gives you the chance to get feedback. What are the things that went well? What could be improved? Now is the time to ask.

> ![Lindsey Kopacz profile image](../Images/736c8ce7e34aa8ee8aeb5831a006d4ae.png)林赛·科帕奇@ little kope 0903![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)对我个人有帮助的事情其实就是写个剧本出来。我觉得[@ editing mily](https://twitter.com/editingemily)也是这样。即使我没有完全按照我的脚本写，写出来的艺术确实帮助我弄清楚每张幻灯片我想要什么样的基调。例如...2019 年 3 月 12 日下午 10 点- 13 点[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105803399465979904)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105803399465979904)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105803399465979904)

> -有什么笑话从技术上帮助我理解了这一点？2019 年 3 月 12 日下午 11 点- 13 日[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105803686482243584)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105803686482243584)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105803686482243584)

回到记忆你的演讲，确保你的演讲流畅并有主题的最好方法之一是写一个脚本。在这次演讲中，你真正想说的是什么？脚本还可以帮助你找到内容中的缺陷或缺口。一旦你有了你的剧本，你可以记住一些关键的短语或要点，但你不一定需要把整本书都记下来。

此外，你可以把你的剧本或大纲带上舞台，作为再次保证。然而，重要的是不要摆弄文件，因为这会非常分散注意力。

> ![Peter Merholz profile image](../Images/fac154804bbcbb021aebe2979f0b848d.png)彼得·默霍尔茨@彼得姆![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)[@ cwodtke](https://twitter.com/cwodtke)以一个故事开始。第一次发言的人经常从原则/抽象开始。你的观众不知道你知道什么，故事可以帮助他们了解，给他们一个参考点。2019 年 3 月 14 日上午 02:21[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1106017449151881216)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1106017449151881216)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1106017449151881216)

Starting with a story is a great hook to grab the attention of your audience. It will allow them to immerse themselves in the content matter. When you jump straight in to technical details, you lose the audience (except those already familiar with the subject matter). So take the time to find a relatable story.

> ![Nate Taylor profile image](../Images/c471435ab8f5e240d33041a12f71c9ea.png)内特·泰勒@泰勒![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)哦，我总是这样告诉一个新人:
> 
> 除了你自己没人知道你要说什么。如果你跳过了一点，不要烦恼或道歉，因为你的观众根本不知道。2019 年 3 月 13 日下午 19:49[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105918896111001602)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105918896111001602)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105918896111001602)

Forget to mention a small detail? No big deal! Your audience wasn't even aware that you missed it! Just keep going.

# [](#technical-details)技术细节

> ![Raphael Almeida profile image](../Images/8b4394b550dff8bdd716146fea79bd7b.png)拉斐尔·阿尔梅达@拉斐尔 _ 阿尔梅达![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)训练不要用活代码，截屏更好防不测2019 年 3 月 13 日下午 13:58[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105830523245654016)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105830523245654016)

Live coding is a great way to engage your audience, but can also quickly go south. Although I have seen some great live coding, it's extremely easy for something to go wrong and screw up your confidence. Including coding screen shots or GIFs is a great way to provide the security of working code while still giving the audience a look into your process.

> ![Henk Pelk profile image](../Images/f4ca5996aea208feceec1ed3c90b2d70.png)亨克佩尔克[@亨德里克佩尔克](https://dev.to/hendrikpelk)![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)有一玻璃杯/一瓶水。我说话时总是口干舌燥！2019 年 3 月 13 日 12 点 52 分[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105813923515826177)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105813923515826177)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105813923515826177)

You might not think you'll need a bottle of water up on stage, but it's better to be safe than sorry. More often than not you'll get some nerves up there so it's best to have some water by your side. Not only can water help if you suffer from dry mouth, but it can also provide a short little break when you need to reset your mind.

> ![Jessica Rannow profile image](../Images/19d10e2aede5e6ea05c4443206ccfd5b.png)杰西卡·兰诺@杰西卡 _ 兰诺![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)穿你以前穿过的衣服和舒适的鞋子。我见过演讲者对粘在衬衫上的项链、坐在舞台上不好看的裙子等有意见。你的演讲已经够让你担心的了；不要让你的服装引起焦虑！2019 年 3 月 13 日下午 18:43[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105902225581371392)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105902225581371392)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105902225581371392)

Wearing a comfortable outfit will be key to your success. If you aren't comfortable with what you're wearing, it will distract you from delivering your best talk.

# [](#cadence)抑扬顿挫

> ![Kelly Vaughn 💫 profile image](../Images/0ed7ef70e3e8cf378cbbfaa4a47e3076.png)凯莉·沃恩💫@ kvlly![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)句子之间停顿的时间比你认为需要的时间长。对观众来说这听起来很自然，你可以更好地调整自己的节奏。你不会记得你准备的所有东西，这没关系。我想我记得第一次演讲时我想说的 75%的内容。2019 年 3 月 13 日 12:11 点[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105803648838393857)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105803648838393857)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105803648838393857)

Pausing between sentences or phrases is important because it allows the audience to digest the last pieces of information. When you skip from one statement to the next without a break, it can be extremely hard for audience members to process the information.

> ![Kelly Vaughn 💫 profile image](../Images/0ed7ef70e3e8cf378cbbfaa4a47e3076.png)凯莉·沃恩💫@ kvlly![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)利用这一点。例如，如果你要做 45 分钟的演讲，准备 60 分钟的内容。除非你绝对需要，否则我不建议你做笔记。让你的幻灯片引导谈话。当你在舞台上的时候，音符就成了你的拐杖。2019 年 3 月 12 日 12 点 12 分[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105803882884710402)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105803882884710402)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105803882884710402)

Sometimes your talk will finish much quicker than you anticipated, so it's totally fine to prepare some extra content which will provide additional benefit. Just don't rush through the main content to get to the auxiliary content.

> ![Kelly Vaughn 💫 profile image](../Images/0ed7ef70e3e8cf378cbbfaa4a47e3076.png)凯莉·沃恩💫@ kvlly![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)前 20 秒最难。但是一旦你开始说话，时间就会飞逝。没有人会注意到你的错误。没人知道你想说什么。不要为你认为是错误的事情道歉。2019 年 3 月 13 日下午 12 点 13 分[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105804057925636107)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105804057925636107)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105804057925636107)

The time will fly much more quickly on stage than you anticipate. You are your greatest naysayer, so don't fret if you make a grammar mistake or stumble on your words a bit. The audience most likely won't notice all these little mistakes.

> ![Peter as a Service profile image](../Images/70fd452aad9adcd34edfd86aeede886c.png)彼得作为服务@ petervandijck![twitter logo](../Images/65e26e35707d96169ec8af6b3cbf2003.png)[@ EmmaWedekind](https://twitter.com/EmmaWedekind)慢慢说，插入 10 秒停顿。寻找感兴趣的人，和他们交谈。2019 年 3 月 13 日 13 点 18 分[![Twitter reply action](../Images/5d5a32424597af8488f7190c7d7d496b.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?in_reply_to=1105820533986332673)[![Twitter retweet action](../Images/3d12d4a909b79beaf8d81b6491fd052d.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/retweet?tweet_id=1105820533986332673)[![Twitter like action](../Images/3f89df2f36e8e5624d2a25952b3ac8b8.png)](https://twitter.com/intent/like?tweet_id=1105820533986332673)

By nature, you will always speak more quickly than you think you will. Thus, it's important to be mindful of how quickly you're speaking and take breaks in between key phrases. You can even find a friendly face or two in the audience to look to for reassurance.

No matter how seasoned you are as a speaker, you will most likely get nervous before a talk. Take that nervous energy and use it to your advantage!

If you aren't enjoying your talk, neither will the audience. So it's important to showcase your enthusiasm for the subject matter. And never self-depreciate! You have nothing to apologize for.
